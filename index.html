Progress likes to tell stories. It favors invention, breakthrough, inevitability. It points to new materials and says: Look what we've made. It rarely asks: What did this replace? What did it erase?

But they carried consequences that were not failures of chemistry. They were failures of relationship. We imitate nature. We abstract its patterns, model its systems, reproduce its effects in machines and markets. But imitation without belonging is not understanding. It is mimicry as control. Simulation without reciprocity.

This archive does not offer a solution. It's not a warning. It's a record of how modern systems learned to use matter without memory.

What do we know when we're no longer embedded in what we're using? What remains when a material outlives the system it served?
