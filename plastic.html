Plastic as Promise
1869–1945: A moral invention. Plastic mimicked ivory, coral, shell — without taking life.
The Rise of Synthetic Ubiquity
1950–1975: Mass production begins. Packaging. Convenience. Disposability. Growth becomes exponential.
In 1950, 2 million tons of plastic were produced.
The Age of Accumulation
1980–2025: Waste outpaces reuse. Recycling myths collapse. Enzymes and fungi try to fix what humans can't undo.
Legacy of a Material
Plastic does not biodegrade. It fragments. It lingers. It circulates in water, soil, even blood.
