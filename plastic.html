Matter Without Memory

Progress likes to tell stories. It favors invention, breakthrough, inevitability. It rarely asks: What did this replace? What did it erase?

They carried consequences that were not failures of chemistry. They were failures of relationship.

This archive does not offer a solution. It's a record of how modern systems learned to use matter without memory.

What do we know when we're no longer embedded in what we're using?
